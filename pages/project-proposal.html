Purpose & Motivation

Why Space Debris?

Space debris refers to defunct human-made objects in orbit — old satellites, spent rocket stages, and collision fragments — whose numbers continue to grow unchecked. While engineers routinely optimize for performance and efficiency, insufficient attention is paid to disposal and long-term environmental impact. The accumulation of space debris is a direct consequence of that gap. Our project examines this issue through engineering ethics, arguing that responsible engineering must account for the full lifecycle of a product — not just its design and deployment.

Audience

Who This Is For

Our primary audience is aerospace engineers and policymakers involved in space governance. We also aim to reach engineering students, industry professionals, and the general public. Space debris is not only a technical problem — it is an ethical responsibility owed to the future of humanity. Commercial incentives and industry competitiveness often override long-term sustainability, even when engineers recognize the risks. Our goal is to make those conflicts visible and to motivate constructive dialogue about the ethical gaps in current regulatory practice.

Deliverables

Implementation Plan

Our group will produce a 3,000-word commentary analyzing space debris as an engineering ethics issue. The commentary will cover background and problem definition, ethical analysis, real-world case studies, and policy and engineering recommendations.
