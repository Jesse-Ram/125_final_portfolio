Following Independent Journalism — Johnny Harris

Johnny Harris is an independent journalist and filmmaker with over 7 million subscribers whose work sits at the intersection of geopolitics, history, and investigative storytelling. His videos expose corruption, trace the hidden forces behind international conflicts, and challenge the narratives fed to us by governments and media institutions. Following his work is a deliberate practice in sharpening my ethical imagination, learning to ask who benefits, who is harmed, and what is being hidden are questions that apply just as much to engineering decisions as they do to foreign policy. His approach to researching and presenting complex systems mirrors the kind of critical thinking this course has pushed me to develop, and I plan to continue engaging with his work as one way of staying curious and skeptical beyond the classroom.

Part B

Resources to Explore

A curated list of books, journals, organizations, and other resources I plan to engage with going forward.

Books

01
Weapons of Math Destruction by Cathy O'Neil — Explores how algorithms and data models quietly shape lives — hiring, lending, policing — often hurting the most vulnerable. A wake-up call for any engineer who builds systems that touch people.
02
Range: Why Generalists Triumph in a Specialized World by David Epstein — Argues that broad curiosity and diverse experience make you more creative and adaptable than deep early specialization. A compelling case for exploring outside your lane — personally and professionally.

Journals & Publications

01
Science and Engineering Ethics Journal — Peer-reviewed articles on engineering ethics cases and theory.
02
Journal of Military Ethics — Covers the moral dimensions of warfare, including emerging debates on autonomous weapons and AI-guided drone systems. Relevant for understanding where engineering decisions intersect with life-and-death consequences.
03
Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists — Originally founded by Manhattan Project scientists grappling with the consequences of their work, it now covers AI risks, autonomous weapons, and the systemic dangers of technology in conflict. A sobering and essential read for any engineer.

Professional Societies

01
AIAA (American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics) — The leading professional society for aerospace and aeronautical engineers. Beyond technical conferences and publications, AIAA has working groups on ethics, autonomous systems, and the responsible development of aerospace technology — directly relevant given the growing role of drones and AI in aviation. Student membership is available and includes access to their digital library and local section events.

Commitments I Can Start This Week

01
Subscribe to MIT Technology Review — A free newsletter covering AI, ethics, and emerging tech. Takes 10 minutes to read and keeps my ethics antennae sharp without a big time commitment.
02
Join AIAA as a student member — Student membership is low cost and gives access to publications, local chapter events, and the ethics community right away.
