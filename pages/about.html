Background

I am a mechanical engineering student at UC Berkeley interested in aerospace, propulsion, and the kind of engineering work that involves real hardware and real constraints. The areas I gravitate toward are thermal systems, fluids, combustion, controls, and mechatronics. I like understanding how things actually work, not just how to solve the problem set version of them.

I tend to learn best when I can connect a concept to a system or a situation where it matters. When something only makes sense on paper I keep pulling at it until it makes sense in practice too.

After Graduation

I will be joining Boeing as an engineer after graduation. I am genuinely excited about it and also aware that it comes with real responsibility. Boeing builds systems where engineering decisions have direct consequences for safety and public trust. That is part of why this course mattered to me. Knowing the FEREs, understanding how workplace culture shapes what engineers actually do, and being able to recognize when a technical decision carries ethical weight are not soft skills. They are things I will need from day one.

This Portfolio

This portfolio is a record of one semester of trying to think more carefully about what it means to do engineering well. The space debris project, the EiN analysis, and the reflections here are connected by the same question the course kept asking: who is affected by the work engineers do, and what do we owe them?

I do not have a perfect answer to that yet. But I have better tools for thinking about it than I did in January.
